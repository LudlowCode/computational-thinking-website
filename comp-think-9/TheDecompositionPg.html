In computing, decomposition is the process of breaking down a complex problem or system into smaller, more manageable parts. It's also known as factoring.

Decomposition

is a key part of computational thinking and is considered an effective way to solve complex problems. It helps identify patterns, eliminate unnecessary details, and solve problems step by step.

Here are some benefits of decomposition:

Easier to understand: The smaller parts are easier to understand and work with. Identify patterns: Decomposition helps identify patterns in the problem. Eliminate unnecessary details: Decomposition helps eliminate extraneous details from the problem.

Solve step by step:

Decomposition helps solve the problem step by step instead of trying to do it all at once.

For example,

you can decompose the problem of making dinner into smaller problems like:

  1. a starter,
  2. main course,
  3. and dessert.

You can then break down the main course further into

  • meat

    • and

  • vegetables.

In computer science,

algorithms can also be decomposed into simpler steps, such as data cleaning, feature selection, model training, and result evaluation.
