Decomposition is the first step, where a large problem is divided into smaller, simpler parts. By breaking down a task into its components, you can focus on solving each piece individually. This method reduces complexity and allows you to tackle each part separately.
For example, if you're planning a party, decomposition would involve separating tasks like sending invitations, preparing food, setting up decorations, and organizing activities.



Abstraction is stripping away unnecessary details and focusing only on the important information needed to solve the problem. By ignoring irrelevant elements, you can simplify the process
and concentrate on the core aspects of the task. In an escape room, for instance, abstraction would involve focusing on the clues and puzzles that matter, rather than being
distracted by props or items that donâ€™t contribute to solving the challenge.



Pattern recognition is the next step, where you look for similarities, trends, or repeating elements in the problem. This step allows you to apply solutions from past experiences
or identify shortcuts when familiar structures emerge. Recognizing patterns is essential in solving puzzles or tasks efficiently, such as noticing that certain codes or
locks in an escape room follow similar rules, allowing you to reuse solutions that have worked before.



Algorithms involve creating a step-by-step process to solve the problem. Algorithms provide a structured approach to completing tasks, ensuring that you follow a consistent method.
In cooking, for instance, a recipe serves as an algorithm, guiding you from start to finish with clear instructions.
Algorithms help ensure that nothing is overlooked, and they make the problem-solving process repeatable.
